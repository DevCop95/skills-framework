This skill guides creation of distinctive, production-grade frontend interfaces that avoid generic "AI slop" aesthetics while maintaining strict professional design constraints.

The user provides frontend requirements: a component, page, application, or interface to build. They may include context about the purpose, audience, or technical constraints.

---

## 🚫 Hard Visual Constraints (NON-NEGOTIABLE)

The following are strictly forbidden:

- ❌ Gradients of any kind  
- ❌ Neon colors or glow effects  
- ❌ Purple as dominant or accent color  
- ❌ Overly dark interfaces (black-heavy or near-black UIs)  

If requested:
- Do NOT implement them  
- Redirect to a professional alternative  

---

## Design Thinking

Before coding, understand the context and commit to a BOLD but professional aesthetic direction:

- **Purpose**: What problem does this interface solve? Who uses it?
- **Tone**: Choose a strong direction BUT grounded in professional aesthetics (refined minimalism, editorial, corporate elegance, industrial, etc.)
- **Constraints**: Technical requirements (framework, performance, accessibility)
- **Differentiation**: What makes this interface memorable WITHOUT relying on visual gimmicks?

**CRITICAL**: Creativity must come from structure, typography, spacing, and composition — NOT from effects like gradients or neon.

---

## Frontend Aesthetics Guidelines

Focus on:

### Typography
- Choose distinctive, high-quality fonts
- Avoid generic fonts (Arial, Inter, Roboto)
- Pair expressive headings with refined body fonts

---

### Color & Theme

- Use **solid colors only** (NO gradients)
- Use professional palettes:
  - Blues
  - Warm neutrals
  - Soft grays
  - Controlled accent colors

- Avoid:
  - Purple-heavy palettes
  - Oversaturated colors
  - Neon tones

---

### Motion

- Use subtle, intentional animations
- Focus on:
  - Fade
  - Small transitions
  - Smooth hover effects

Avoid:
- Glow effects  
- Flashy animations  
- Neon-like interactions  

---

### Spatial Composition

- Use strong layout decisions:
  - Asymmetry (controlled)
  - Clean grids
  - Intentional whitespace

Avoid:
- Chaotic layouts
- Overlapping noise
- Visual clutter

---

### Backgrounds & Visual Details

- Prefer solid or lightly textured backgrounds
- Use depth through spacing and layering

Avoid:
- Gradient backgrounds  
- Neon lighting effects  
- Overly dark surfaces  

---

## Implementation Rules

All generated code must be:

- Production-ready  
- Clean and structured  
- Visually consistent  
- Accessible (WCAG AA minimum contrast)  

---

## Anti-Patterns (NEVER DO)

- Purple gradients on light backgrounds  
- Neon glowing buttons  
- Black-heavy UI with low contrast  
- Overdesigned "AI-looking" layouts
